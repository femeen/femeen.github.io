Is feminism only for women?

There are many reasons why people decide to go against feminism. I common reason is because some people believe that feminism is a movement against men. That feminist strive to make women above men not equal to men. Other people don't think that feminism doesn’t benefit men in any way. However, feminism is a movement for men and women to be equal to one another socially, politically and economically. In fact equality between men and women benefit both genders in many different ways. Given that women make up almost half of the workforce, their integration had huge positive ripple effects in all industries. Feminism has also successfully overturned laws that discriminated against men. For example the case, Craig v. Boren, was filled by a plaintiff in Oklahoma over its gender-specific drinking age policy, which prohibited men from drinking before age 21, but allowed women to drink when as young as 18. This implied that men are more reckless and women are more responsible. After the law was struck down, the drinking age became 21 for all. Another example, is that until recently the FBI’s definition of rape was old-fashioned and only including a man violating a women. That is, until feminist activists decided to change that. Thanks to the "Rape Is Rape" campaign launched by the Feminist Majority Foundation and Ms. magazine, more than 160,000 emails were sent to the FBI pressuring it to change its archaic definition of rape. The old definition, "carnal knowledge of a female forcibly and against her will," hadn't been changed since 1921. It meant that many types of sexual assaults, including the rape of men, weren't counted as part of the bureau's annual Uniform Crime Report. When the decision was announced, then-VP and General Counsel of the Feminist Majority Foundation Kim Gandy said, "This is a major policy change and will dramatically impact the way rape is tracked and reported nationwide." Feminism doesn't only benefit women, it benefits people of all characters in different ways. Most people who are against feminism don't know the proper definition or have a misinterpretation of what feminism is.
